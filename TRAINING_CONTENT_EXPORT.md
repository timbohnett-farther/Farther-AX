## 01 — Introduction

**Page Title:** Welcome to the AX Playbook
**Subtitle:** Advisor Experience Onboarding & Transition Reference
**Step:** 01 / 13

### Overview

This playbook is the definitive reference for Farther's Advisor Experience (AX) team. It guides **AX Managers (AXMs)** and **AX Associates (AXAs)** through every step of bringing a new advisor onto the Farther platform — from the moment a deal is signed through the advisor's graduation to full independence. Whether you're onboarding a breakaway advisor leaving a wirehouse, integrating an Independent RIA, or working through a complex M&A situation, this resource provides the frameworks, documents, timelines, and workflows you need.

### The Four Advisor Onboarding Paths

Every advisor follows one of four distinct paths based on their background and book of business.

#### Path 01: Breakaway (Wirehouse / Captive Firm)

**Badge:** High Compliance

Advisor departing a wirehouse or captive firm. Highest compliance sensitivity — resignation must occur before certain steps can proceed, and the U4 is held until departure. Typical advisors leaving Merrill Lynch, Morgan Stanley, UBS, or Wells Fargo.

#### Path 02: Independent RIA (Existing RIA Owner)

**Badge:** Lower Legal Risk

Advisor who already owns their book of business and operates independently. Must file an ADV-W within 90 days of joining Farther. Considerations vary by dual vs. non-dual registration states.

#### Path 03: M&A (Mergers & Acquisitions)

**Badge:** Case-by-Case

Advisor or practice joining Farther through an acquisition or merger transaction. Complex legal and operational considerations apply. Each situation is handled individually with dedicated support.

#### Path 04: No to Low AUM (Below $15–20M AUM)

**Badge:** Training First

Advisors bringing less than $15–20M in assets under management. A training-first approach is taken before full onboarding begins. Requires a Focus Team assessment to evaluate fit and growth trajectory.

### The Three Transition Methods

After an advisor joins, client assets must transition to Farther's custodians via one of three methods.

#### Method 1: Master Merge

**Timeline:** 4–6 weeks
**Label:** Fastest Method

Assets are moved via a master account merge at the custodian level. Best suited for advisors with large, uniform books of business. Requires close custodian coordination and is the most efficient path when available.

#### Method 2: LPOA — Limited Power of Attorney

**Timeline:** 6–8 weeks
**Label:** Mid-Range Timeline

The advisor signs an LPOA document authorizing Farther to act on behalf of clients. Available at Schwab, Fidelity IWS, and Pershing PAS. A balanced option when a Master Merge is not available.

#### Method 3: Repaper / ACAT

**Timeline:** 8–12 weeks
**Label:** Longest Method

Clients must sign new account paperwork (repaper) and assets transfer via ACAT — Automated Customer Account Transfer. Required when neither LPOA nor Master Merge is available. The most client-touch-intensive path.

## 02 — Onboarding vs. Transitions

**Page Title:** Onboarding vs. Transitions
**Subtitle:** Understanding Team Ownership & Role Responsibilities
**Step:** 02 / 13

### Two Distinct Workstreams

The AX team operates two parallel workstreams when a new advisor joins Farther.

Onboarding and Transitions are distinct processes with different owners, timelines, and success metrics. Understanding the boundary between these two workstreams is critical to ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

#### Workstream 1: Onboarding

Owned by the **AX Manager (AXM)**.

Covers everything related to setting up the advisor as a Farther professional — their technology access, compliance filings, training, introductions to internal teams, and graduation milestones.

#### Workstream 2: Transitions

Owned by the **Transitions team (CTM/CTA)**.

Covers the movement of client assets from the advisor's previous custodian to Farther's custodians. This process runs in parallel with onboarding but has its own timeline and dependencies.

### AXM — Advisor Experience Manager

**Primary Ownership:** Onboarding workstream
**Reports to:** Head of Advisor Experience

#### Core Responsibilities

- Serve as the primary point of contact for the incoming advisor from deal-signed through graduation
- Coordinate the onboarding kickoff meeting (typically within 48 hours of deal signed)
- Manage all compliance filings: U4 submission, ADV-W (for Independent RIAs), NCL processing
- Ensure advisor completes all required training modules before launch
- Run the weekly check-in cadence throughout the onboarding period
- Own the "Go Live" milestone and graduation criteria
- Escalate issues to legal, compliance, or executive sponsors as needed
- Maintain accurate records in the Transition Tracker

#### Key Relationships

Advisor • AXA • Compliance team • Legal team • Technology/Ops • CTM

#### Success Metrics

- Advisor launches on or ahead of target date
- All compliance documents filed on time
- Advisor NPS / satisfaction score post-graduation
- No compliance violations during the onboarding window

### AXA — Advisor Experience Associate

**Primary Ownership:** Day-to-day execution and logistics support
**Reports to:** AXM

#### Core Responsibilities

- Support the AXM in all onboarding logistics and coordination
- Schedule all required meetings (kickoff, check-ins, training sessions, graduation)
- Manage document collection: gather, review, and track IAA, U4, LPOA, and all required agreements
- Send advisor welcome communications and ensure portal access is set up
- Track open items and follow-ups in the Transition Tracker
- Coordinate with the CTM/CTA on transition timelines and custodian requirements
- Prepare meeting agendas and send follow-up summaries after each session
- Manage the Holiday List distribution and scheduling considerations

#### Key Relationships

AXM • CTM • CTA • Advisor • Advisor's team/admin

#### Success Metrics

- All required documents collected before kickoff
- No missed meetings or scheduling gaps
- Transition Tracker kept current (updated within 24 hours of each touchpoint)

### The Handoff Moment

**Timeline Overview:**

- **Deal Signed** → **Transition Initiated** → **Go Live** → **Graduation**

**Onboarding track** — runs Deal Signed → Graduation

**Transitions track** — runs Transition Initiated → account transfers close

**Onboarding begins at deal-signed.** Transitions begin when the advisor's chosen transition method is confirmed and custodian paperwork is initiated. Both workstreams proceed in parallel until Go Live, at which point onboarding continues through graduation while transitions closes out remaining account transfers.

### Document Definitions

#### 01 — IAA (Investment Advisory Agreement)

The foundational contract between the advisor and Farther. Establishes the legal relationship, fee schedule, investment discretion, and terms of service. Must be executed by the advisor before onboarding can proceed.

**Owner:** Legal / Compliance
**Timing:** Before kickoff meeting

#### 02 — U4 (Uniform Application for Securities Registration)

FINRA's uniform registration form. Required for all advisors who hold securities licenses (Series 65, 66, 7, etc.). For Breakaway advisors, the U4 is held (not submitted) until after the advisor has formally resigned from their previous firm to avoid triggering a "U4 event."

**Owner:** Compliance
**Timing:** Submitted after resignation (Breakaway) or at kickoff (others)

#### 03 — ADV-W (Withdrawal of Investment Adviser Registration)

Filed with the SEC or state regulators to formally withdraw an advisor's registration as an independent investment adviser. Required for Independent RIA advisors who are dissolving their own RIA to join Farther. Must be filed within 90 days of joining Farther.

**Owner:** Advisor (with Compliance support)
**Timing:** Within 90 days of Go Live

#### 04 — NCL (Non-Compete / Non-Solicitation Letter)

A letter acknowledging the advisor's obligations under any non-compete or non-solicitation agreements with their prior firm. Breakaway advisors in particular must be careful about client communication before resignation. The NCL documents what the advisor has agreed to and protects Farther.

**Owner:** Legal
**Timing:** Before advisor resignation (Breakaway)

#### 05 — LPOA Document (Limited Power of Attorney)

Authorizes Farther to act on behalf of clients when transitioning assets. The advisor signs the LPOA (not the clients), which enables the custodian to move assets without requiring individual client signatures. Available at Schwab, Fidelity IWS, and Pershing PAS.

**Owner:** AXM / Transitions team
**Timing:** During LPOA transition method initiation

#### 06 — Holiday List

A calendar of market holidays and custodian processing blackout dates. Critical for scheduling ACAT submissions, account openings, and key transition milestones. Distributed to the advisor's team at the start of transitions.

**Owner:** AXA
**Timing:** Provided at kickoff

#### 07 — Transition Spreadsheet

The master tracking document for all client accounts being transitioned. Lists every account by client name, account number, custodian, account type, and transition status. Updated by the CTM/CTA as accounts transfer. The AXM reviews weekly.

**Owner:** CTM (primary), AXM (review)
**Timing:** Created at transition kickoff, maintained through Go Live

#### 08 — ACATs (Automated Customer Account Transfers)

The electronic system used by DTCC (and custodians) to transfer customer accounts between broker-dealers. ACATs are required for the Repaper/ACAT transition method. Each account transfer is initiated via ACAT request.

**Owner:** Transitions team (CTM / CTA)
**Timing:** After new accounts are opened and paperwork is signed

#### 09 — Exhibit B

An addendum to the IAA that specifies the advisor's compensation structure, payout rate, and any special arrangements. Confidential and specific to each advisor. Must be signed alongside the IAA.

**Owner:** Finance / Legal
**Timing:** Same as IAA

## 04 — Breakaway

**Page Title:** Breakaway
**Subtitle:** Advisor Pathway — Wirehouse & Captive Firm Departures
**Step:** 04 / 13

### Introduction

The Breakaway pathway applies to advisors departing a wirehouse (Merrill Lynch, Morgan Stanley, UBS, Wells Fargo, Raymond James, etc.) or other captive firm to join Farther. This pathway carries the **highest compliance sensitivity** of all four onboarding paths. Every step must be carefully sequenced to protect the advisor, protect Farther, and avoid triggering unnecessary legal action from the departing firm.

### Key Characteristics

#### Compliance Sensitivity: High

Non-solicitation and non-compete agreements are common. Advisors may face legal action from their prior firm if they contact clients before resignation. AXM and Legal must be briefed before any advisor communication goes out.

#### U4 Timing: Hold Until Resignation

The U4 registration form must NOT be submitted until after the advisor has formally resigned. Submitting early creates a trackable FINRA event that can alert the prior firm prematurely.

#### Client Communication: Restricted Pre-Resignation

Under the Protocol for Broker Recruitment, advisors may take only client names, addresses, phone numbers, email, and account titles. Any broader client data extraction is prohibited. Advisors should consult their prior firm's protocol membership status.

#### Resignation Required: Mandatory First Step

The advisor must formally resign before the Farther onboarding can fully proceed. The AXM coordinates timing with the advisor and Legal to minimize exposure and ensure the transition is clean.

### Protocol for Broker Recruitment

**⚠️ PROTOCOL-PERMITTED DATA ONLY**

Farther operates under the Protocol for Broker Recruitment. Advisors leaving a protocol member firm may bring **only** the following:

1. Client name
2. Address
3. Phone number
4. Email address
5. Account title

Advisors must verify their prior firm's protocol status before departure. Non-protocol situations require additional Legal review.

### Critical Timeline Sequencing

#### 01 — Deal Signed

AXM assigned. Legal briefed. NCL reviewed. No client contact yet.

#### 02 — Pre-Resignation Phase

All Farther tech access and training completed in stealth. IAA and Exhibit B signed. U4 prepared but held.

#### 03 — Resignation Day

Advisor formally resigns. U4 submitted immediately. Client notification letters prepared for same-day or next-day delivery.

#### 04 — Post-Resignation

Client outreach begins per protocol. Transition method initiated. Custodian paperwork submitted.

#### 05 — Go Live

Advisor is active on Farther platform. Transitions running in parallel.

### Common Pitfalls

- Submitting U4 before resignation — creates FINRA event, alerts prior firm
- Client outreach before resignation — non-solicitation violation risk
- Taking client data beyond protocol list — grounds for injunctive relief
- Failing to check prior firm's protocol membership status
- Not briefing Farther Legal before advisor communicates any transition plans

## 05 — Independent RIA

**Page Title:** Independent RIA
**Subtitle:** Advisor Pathway — Independent Registered Investment Advisers
**Step:** 05 / 13

### Introduction

The Independent RIA pathway applies to advisors who are already operating as registered independent investment advisers — meaning they own their own book of business and have their own SEC or state RIA registration. This is typically the **lowest-risk pathway** from a legal standpoint, but it has unique regulatory requirements that must be addressed promptly.

### Key Characteristics

#### Compliance Sensitivity: Lower

No wirehouse non-solicitation concerns. The advisor owns their book and client relationships. Client communication is generally unrestricted.

#### ADV-W Required: Within 90 Days

The advisor must file Form ADV-W (withdrawal of investment adviser registration) within 90 days of joining Farther. This formally dissolves their independent RIA registration with the SEC or state regulator.

#### Dual vs. Non-Dual Registration: State-Dependent

Some states require dual registration (both SEC and state). The compliance requirements and ADV-W process differ based on the advisor's registration type and the states where their clients reside.

#### Book Ownership: Advisor-Owned

Since the advisor owns their client relationships, the transition of clients to Farther is typically smoother than Breakaway. No protocol constraints apply.

### ADV-W Filing Requirements

**ℹ️ 90-DAY REGULATORY DEADLINE**

Form ADV-W must be filed within **90 days of the advisor's Go Live date** on Farther. Failure to file creates a regulatory gap where the advisor is simultaneously registered as an independent RIA and affiliated with Farther. The AXM must track this deadline and confirm filing with the advisor and Compliance.

### Dual vs. Non-Dual Registration

#### Dual Registration States

States like New York and California may require separate state-level registration in addition to SEC registration. ADV-W must be filed with both the SEC (via IARD) and the applicable state regulator.

#### Non-Dual Registration States

SEC-registered RIAs in these states only need to file with the SEC via IARD. State-level ADV-W is not required.

### Transition Considerations

- Clients follow the advisor to Farther — inform clients of the move to Farther and the new custodian/account structure
- No protocol restrictions on client communication
- LPOA or Repaper/ACAT are the most common transition methods for Independent RIAs
- Master Merge is rare but possible depending on prior custodian arrangements

## 06 — M&A

**Page Title:** M&A
**Subtitle:** Advisor Pathway — Mergers & Acquisitions
**Step:** 06 / 13

### Why This Matters at Farther

Farther's growth strategy is built on combining top-tier advisor talent with proprietary technology — and RIA acquisitions are a meaningful accelerant to that mission. When Farther brings on an acquired book of business, the Advisor Experience and Onboarding team sits at the intersection of every workstream: compliance, technology migration, client retention, and cultural integration. Understanding the fundamentals of RIA acquisitions isn't optional — it's how this team protects the firm, the advisors, and most importantly, the clients.

### Part 1: How Farther Evaluates an RIA Before We Buy

Before our team touches a deal, it has already gone through a rigorous six-phase evaluation process. Knowing where a deal stands in this pipeline helps the Onboarding team anticipate what's coming and prepare accordingly.

#### Phase 1: Initial Assessment
**Lead:** M&A Strategist
**Question:** Is this a strategic fit? Go/No-Go for full diligence?

#### Phase 2: Financial Deep Dive
**Lead:** Financial Due Diligence
**Question:** Is the revenue real, sustainable, and clean?

#### Phase 3: Regulatory & Compliance Review
**Lead:** Regulatory Architect
**Question:** Any legal landmines? ADV issues? Exam history?

#### Phase 4: Operations & Technology
**Lead:** Tech & Ops Analyst
**Question:** How hard is the tech migration? What does it cost?

#### Phase 5: Client & Advisor Analysis
**Lead:** Client Relationship Strategist
**Question:** Will clients and advisors stay?

#### Phase 6: Integration Planning
**Lead:** Project Manager
**Question:** What does Day 1 through Month 12 look like?

**💡 Key Insight:** By the time the Advisor Experience team is activated, Phases 1–5 are largely complete. **Your work begins in earnest at Phase 6** — and everything you do either validates or undermines the thesis built in Phases 1–5.

### Part 2: Understanding the Deal Structure

#### How RIA Acquisitions Are Valued

RIA books of business are typically valued as a multiple of recurring revenue or seller's discretionary earnings. A proper valuation accounts for revenue quality, client demographics, tenure, attrition risk, and numerous other factors. No two books are identical — context matters enormously.

#### Earnouts: The Deal Structure You'll Encounter Most

Nearly every RIA acquisition at scale includes an **earnout**, where a portion of the purchase price is deferred and paid based on post-close performance. This is critically important for the Onboarding team to understand because **how we handle the transition directly affects whether earnout targets are met.**

**Earnout Statistics:**

- **20–35%** of total purchase price now structured as earnout
- **1–3 years** typical earnout period; beyond 3 years creates strain
- **AUM + Revenue** common performance triggers alongside client retention
- **Buyer obligation** failure to deliver promised support can legally impair earnout

**⚠️ Onboarding team takeaway:** A delayed technology migration, a poor client communication rollout, or a rocky first 90 days can directly reduce earnout payments — and damage Farther's reputation as an acquirer.

### Part 3: Compliance — What the Onboarding Team Must Know

Compliance is the highest-stakes area for any RIA acquisition. Errors here have regulatory, financial, and reputational consequences.

#### Form ADV — The Foundation

**CORE DOCUMENT**

- **Part 1:** Statistical firm data — AUM, structure, investment strategies, examination history
- **Part 2A:** Brochure — narrative disclosure of services, fees, conflicts of interest, and personnel
- **Part 2B:** Brochure Supplement — background on key advisory personnel
- **Part 3 / CRS:** Client Relationship Summary for SEC-registered firms serving retail investors

**Why it matters for onboarding:** When Farther acquires an RIA, the acquired firm's ADV must be updated or superseded. Clients must receive updated disclosure documents. Any material changes trigger re-disclosure obligations.

#### Regulatory Red Flags to Know Before Day 1

- Unresolved SEC exam deficiencies — must be remediated before closing or on a defined timeline
- Client complaint or arbitration history — pattern complaints suggest systemic service issues
- State registration complexity — multi-state RIAs have layered filing requirements beyond SEC mandates
- Insurance coverage gaps — E&O, Cyber, and Fiduciary coverage must be confirmed and transitioned

#### The 30-Day Advisor Onboarding Compliance Standard

Documentation must be complete, accessible, and defensible before the advisor is deployed. SEC and state examiners do not accept partial onboarding as a defense.

**Days 0–5: Pre-Onboarding**
Prior registrations, licenses, CRD review, U4 preparation, background checks

**Days 5–10: Conflicts & Disclosures**
OBA disclosures, personal trading accounts, Code of Ethics acknowledgment

**Days 10–20: Mandatory Training**
Cybersecurity, compliance fundamentals, email/social media rules — with timestamped completion records

**Days 20–25: Policy Attestations**
Handbook, cybersecurity policy, BCP, advertising rules

**Days 25–30: Supervisory Sign-Off**
Supervisor approval, full archive of onboarding file for exam review

**🚫 Important:** Slow onboarding is not an operational nuisance — it is a compliance failure vector.

#### Special Compliance Concerns at Farther

**Marketing Rule Compliance**
Acquired advisors may have used testimonials, hypothetical performance, or social media in ways that don't comply with SEC Marketing Rule requirements

**Cybersecurity & Data Transfer**
Farther's platform is a closed-loop encrypted ecosystem. Client data from acquired firms must be migrated into this ecosystem — not held in third-party systems

**Custody Rule**
Confirm the acquired RIA's custody arrangements are properly disclosed and structured before migration begins

**Fiduciary Standard Alignment**
Any acquired firm with commission-based compensation structures requires careful review to ensure alignment with Farther's fiduciary model

### Part 4: Client Retention — The Metric That Drives Everything

Client retention is the single most important post-acquisition metric. Earnout payments depend on it. Farther's reputation as an acquirer depends on it.

**Retention Statistics:**

- **86–94%+** Well-executed transition retention
- **97%** 5-year RIA client retention baseline
- **19%** Avg attrition from poor custodian transitions
- **6–9 mo** Pivotal retention window post-acquisition

#### What Actually Drives Retention

1. Communicate with clients before anyone else does — don't let news travel through the grapevine
2. Keep messaging consistent across all clients — every touchpoint reinforces the same narrative
3. Frame the transition around client benefit — better technology, more personalized planning, stronger support
4. Get clients into Farther's service rhythm (reviews, communications, events) as fast as possible

#### The Warm Handoff Standard

**GOLD STANDARD**

- Joint meetings with the seller during the transition period build trust before the seller steps back
- Negotiate seller participation in the transition as part of deal terms — this directly protects retention and earnout performance
- Use Farther's technology — CRM tracking, automated follow-up workflows, and the AI-powered proposal tool — to ensure no client is overlooked

**💡 Key Insight:** The client doesn't follow the firm — they follow the mechanic. The job is to make sure they understand Farther's team *is* their mechanic going forward.

### Part 5: Technology Integration — Farther's Competitive Advantage

#### What Makes Farther's Tech Stack Different

**Home-built platform**
No stitched-together vendor stack — fewer breaking points during migration

**AI Proposal tool**
Acquired advisors can generate personalized client proposals in under 10 minutes

**Closed-loop data**
All client data stays within Farther's encrypted infrastructure; no third-party re-entry required

**3x efficiency**
Advisors can manage books 3x larger than industry average due to workflow gains

#### Technology Migration Risks to Manage

- Vendor lock-in at the acquired firm — legacy CRM or portfolio management contracts may have expensive breakage clauses
- Data integrity during migration — account history, client notes, and preferences must transfer cleanly
- Cybersecurity posture — past breaches or vulnerabilities discovered post-close become Farther's liability
- Custodian transitions are among the highest-risk moments for client attrition

### Part 6: Red Flags Onboarding Should Escalate Immediately

The onboarding team is often the first to interact with acquired advisors and clients at a human level. This gives the team unique visibility into issues that due diligence may not have surfaced. **Escalate immediately if you observe:**

#### Financial Flags (ESCALATE)

- Advisors claiming higher AUM, revenue, or client count than disclosed in due diligence
- Unusual billing arrangements or fee structures that don't align with the disclosed fee schedule
- Clients expressing surprise at fees or services they say they were never told about

#### Compliance Flags (ESCALATE)

- Advisors referencing undisclosed activities (outside business activities, side arrangements)
- Marketing materials, testimonials, or social media posts that weren't reviewed pre-deal
- Any mention of client complaints, disputes, or informal settlements not disclosed pre-close

#### Cultural Flags (MONITOR)

- Advisor dissatisfaction with Farther's fiduciary model or technology requirements
- Key person dependencies — if one advisor holds 80%+ of client relationships, disengagement is catastrophic
- Client relationships that appear transactional rather than advisory — higher attrition risk

### Quick Reference: Key Terms

- **AUM** — Assets Under Management — the primary value driver of any RIA book
- **Form ADV** — RIA registration document; contains disclosure of services, fees, conflicts, and exam history
- **Form CRS** — Client Relationship Summary — required for SEC-registered RIAs serving retail investors
- **Earnout** — Deferred purchase price contingent on post-close performance metrics like AUM and client retention
- **OBA** — Outside Business Activity — any activity outside the advisor's primary registration that must be disclosed
- **U4** — FINRA registration form for individual advisors — must be filed and clean before activation
- **Warm Handoff** — Transition model where selling advisor introduces successor advisor directly to clients over time
- **CRD** — Central Registration Depository — FINRA's database of advisor registration and disciplinary history
- **Fiduciary Standard** — Legal obligation to act in the client's best interest — Farther's non-negotiable baseline
- **IARD** — Investment Adviser Registration Depository — the SEC/state filing system for RIA registration

### The Onboarding Team's North Star

Making the transition so seamless for clients and advisors that they never question their decision to be part of **Farther**.

That means compliance documentation that holds up under exam scrutiny, technology that works from Day 1, communication that is personal and proactive, and a culture introduction that makes advisors feel they've upgraded — not just changed firms.

*The deals are won in due diligence. The value is created — or destroyed — in onboarding.*

## 07 — No to Low AUM

**Subtitle:** Advisor Pathway — Below $15-20M AUM

### Introduction

The No to Low AUM pathway applies to advisors bringing fewer than $15-20 million in assets under management to Farther. While Farther welcomes advisors at all stages, advisors below this threshold follow a modified, training-first onboarding approach before proceeding to full transitions.

### When This Pathway Applies

**ℹ️ Threshold Evaluation**

This pathway is triggered when an advisor's projected AUM at launch is below $15-20M. The exact threshold is assessed case-by-case with input from the Focus Team, which evaluates the advisor's growth trajectory, client quality, and strategic fit.

### Key Characteristics

#### Focus Team Check Required

Before onboarding begins, the Focus Team reviews the advisor's profile to confirm Farther is the right fit and that the advisor has a credible path to growth.

#### Training-First Approach

Rather than leading with transitions, this pathway prioritizes getting the advisor proficient on the Farther platform first. Full transitions may be deferred until the advisor demonstrates platform mastery.

#### Simplified Transition Process

Given lower AUM, transitions are typically simpler. Repaper/ACAT is most common. LPOA may be used if custodian supports it.

#### Growth Plan Required

The AXM works with the advisor to establish a 90-day and 180-day growth plan with clear milestones for AUM growth, client acquisition, and platform utilization.

### The Focus Team Review

The Focus Team is an internal Farther committee that reviews borderline advisor candidates. Their assessment covers the following areas. The AXM must present the advisor's profile to the Focus Team before initiating any onboarding steps.

**Assessment Areas:**

- Current AUM and trajectory
- Client demographics and retention history
- The advisor's prior firm performance record
- Strategic alignment with Farther's growth goals

### Training Curriculum Priority

**Required Training Modules — In Order:**

01. Farther portal navigation and account management
02. Model portfolio selection and implementation
03. Client reporting and communication tools
04. Compliance workflows and document submission
05. Billing and fee management

## 09 — LPOA

**Page Title:** LPOA
**Subtitle:** Transition Method — Limited Power of Attorney (6-8 Weeks)
**Step:** 09 / 13

### Introduction

The LPOA (Limited Power of Attorney) transition method allows Farther to transfer client assets without requiring individual client signatures on new paperwork. Instead, the advisor signs a single LPOA document granting Farther authority to act on behalf of clients. This is a mid-range timeline method — faster than Repaper/ACAT but slower than Master Merge.

### At a Glance

- **Timeline:** 6-8 Weeks
- **Who Signs:** Advisor (not clients)
- **Available At:** Schwab, Fidelity IWS, Pershing PAS

### How LPOA Works

**Process Steps:**

1. Advisor and AXM confirm LPOA eligibility at the advisor's current custodian(s)
2. Transitions team (CTM) prepares the LPOA document package
3. Advisor signs the LPOA document
4. CTM submits LPOA authorization to custodian
5. Custodian reviews and approves the LPOA (typically 5-10 business days)
6. Asset transfers begin systematically, batch by batch
7. Accounts are monitored daily; discrepancies flagged and resolved
8. All accounts confirmed transferred — transitions closes out

### Custodian Availability

#### Schwab

Full LPOA support. Most common for Breakaway advisors with Schwab accounts.

#### Fidelity IWS

Fidelity Institutional Wealth Services supports LPOA. Requires specific form submission through Fidelity's advisor portal.

#### Pershing PAS

Pershing Portfolio Advisory Service supports LPOA through NetX360.

### Key Considerations

- LPOA does NOT require client notification of the transfer (although best practice is to notify)
- Some account types cannot transfer via LPOA (e.g., 401k, certain annuities) — these may require Repaper
- ACATs may still be needed for non-LPOA-eligible accounts within an otherwise LPOA transition
- The LPOA document must be stored in the client file and Transition Tracker
